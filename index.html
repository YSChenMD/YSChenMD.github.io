Profile

Engineering-minded medicine, built around the patient.

I'm a post-baccalaureate medical student at National Chung Hsing University, building on a Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering from National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University. I'm currently completing clinical clerkships across teaching hospitals in central Taiwan.

My work centers on translational medical-device innovation. As first author, I led the design of a translucent, light-permeable silicone hip orthosis that lets newborns receive jaundice phototherapy without interrupting treatment for developmental dysplasia of the hip (DDH) — running the fabrication, optical transmittance testing, and 3D geometric validation myself. Earlier, I characterized PHEMA hydrogels for medical applications.

This work has earned the Platinum Award at Taiwan's National Healthcare Quality Award and gold medals at international invention salons in Tokyo, Moscow, and Taipei. I'm drawn to the meeting point of orthopaedics, neonatal care, and engineering-driven clinical solutions.
